Like a Property? How to price it


Listing prices in the Bay Area can be highly misleading. Many houses are purposefully priced much lower than the market to start a bidding war. In such cases, the listing agents also typically would not disclose their expectation to ensure that potential buyers make wild guesses. Even in other places where listing price indicates what the seller wants, it's a good idea to check if you are getting a good deal. You can simply contact us for our opinion but here is a guide to check yourself.

Use Zillow to find comparable recent sales in the area

Zillow allows you to search for sold listings in the map. Move the map over the area where the listed property is to find comparable sales over the last couple of years. You can even find sales on the same or nearby streets. Narrow your search criteria and zoom in the map to the desired neighborhood. 

Common mistake: ignoring the lot size

Do not use the per sq feet cost for single family homes. Please take the lot size into account especially in California. Find comparables with similar lot size and house size.

Adjust for property condition

There are many factors which are subjective but can affect the house value: the condition (e.g., a remodeled kitchen or bathroom), good views, natural light, proximity to a park or school, a corner lot. Any highway noise can reduce the property value.

Adjust for the time passed and market condition

Say the comparable property went pending 5 months ago (date went pending is more important than date sold). Consider how much the prices have changed since then? Average appreciation in many big desirable cities has been 0.5% per month over the last couple of decades.

Next, consider whether we are in the buyers market or sellers market. If it's the seller's market, there might be 5 other buyers doing an analysis similar to yours and you might need to add an extra amount to make your offer stand out. If it's the buyer's market, you can think about offering lower than what the comps suggest. It's best to leave this part to us.

What to do if the property is overpriced?

If the comp analysis reveals a price which is significantly lower than the listing price, the seller may have unrealistic expectations. In that case, sometimes a good strategy is to let the property stay on the market for some time and wait for price reduction rather than making an offer which is significantly below the listing price. The sellers may need time to face the reality.

How can I avoid a bidding war?

Keep an eye on properties staying on the market for 2 weeks or longer: making an offer with a short expiration on these properties can help you avoid bidding wars.

Why comparables? Can't you just call the listing agent to ask seller price expectation? 

We used to try this when we were new in the business but the exercise was always fruitless. A listing agent we spoke to put it beautifully: you are NOT competing against seller expectations. You are competing against other buyers. The sellers is just going to pick the highest offer with the best terms no matter what his initial "expectation" was. (Relevant only for the Bay Area)

How Much Buyer Agent Commission to Request


    1) When listing agents present the offer to the seller, they present the net figure (purchase price minus buyer agent commission asked). Sellers only care about the net figure, not the breakup between the purchase price and buyer agent commission.

    Example: the following two offers are equivalent for the seller

        -> Purchase price of $1805k with $5k buyer agent commission

        -> Purchase price of $1850k with $5k buyer agent commission

    In both cases, the seller nets 1800k

    To ensure that listing agents don't mess up, we also include a calculation in the offer which says something like: "Our offer of $1805K is equivalent to over $1845k with 2.5% buyer agent commission".


    2) Given this, we have two options:

    (a) Ask the seller only for our low buyer agent commission (and reduce the purchase price accordingly if needed), or even no buyer agent commission. This has the advantage that it will result in lower purchase price and lower annual property taxes for the buyer. It also reduces the county and city transfer tax (for the buyer as well as the seller).

    (b) Ask for the usual 2-3% commission and get a cashback. In this case, please check with your bank if they have any restrictions on the cash back. Also note that we will have to sign a new representation agreement with the 2-3% commission since it needs to be submitted to the seller to claim the buyer agent commission. But we can provide the excess amount as cashback.

Guide to Smooth Closing

Make the initial deposit and avoid common mistakes

Many buyers don't realize that the federal wire system works according to the East Coast time and the same day wire cut off for your bank can be as early as 11am PT if you are in CA. Please be prepared. The wire instructions will be provided by the escrow officer. Their contact is typically listed in the disclosures cover sheet.

Sign all disclosures

This step is (not) fun! You will have to sign all disclosures individually. You can do this either using a tablet/iPad, a PDF editor (we suggest PDFgear which is free), or, by uploading it to your docusign account (but you will need to manually select where you want to put your initial/signature). If a document doesn’t ask for buyer signatures (e.g., an inspection report), you can simply sign the first page to acknowledge you received it.

Finalize your bank ASAP!

Yes, you can shop for rates and go with the bank which is different from the preapproval one. But the bank is the bottleneck in the whole process, so finalize the bank ASAP, confirm that they can handle your close of escrow date, and ask them to order appraisal. 

Shop for home insurance, setup utilities

Home insurance will be required by the bank sooner rather than later. You can get quotes online from various brokers. You can also start utilities in your name effective the closing date: electricity, water, internet, trash, etc.

Prepare for signing with notary

A few days before the closing, the escrow would set you up with the notary to sign all the documents. A mobile notary can even come to your house and work around your schedule. This is all you need to do. You don’t need to be present for the closing/recording and don’t need to take time off work! PS: You would need a government issued ID for the notary.  

Wire the remaining funds, wait for closing and get the keys!

Wire the remaining funds 1-2 days before the closing (per bank instructions). On the day of closing, escrow does the “recording” which officially transfers the house in your name. After the recording, we will let you know how to get the keys. 

How to Sign Disclosure Documents


    Any document we prepare for you (offer, disclosures, representation agreement etc) will always be sent to you via docusign/authentisign. However some documents prepared by the listing agent may simple be a scanned or plain pdf without signing tags. You might have to sign many of these documents which can run over a hundred pages in CA. Here is are some options:


    1. Use a tablet/iPad or simply print, sign and scan. This is the least efficient method but it works.

    2. Use a PDF editor where you can type your name and it will create an initial and a signature for you. Then simply click on the places in the document where you want to put your initial/signature. We suggest PDFgear which is free

    3. Create a (free) docusign account, upload the document, and manually select where you want to put your initial/signature


    If a document doesn’t ask for buyer signatures (e.g., an inspection report), you can simply sign the first page to acknowledge that you received it.

    If you prefer to have all seller provided documents converted to docusign with signature tags, our transaction coordinator can do it for $100.
