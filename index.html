Changing Real Estate

The field of real estate is changing dramatically with the advent of internet and smartphones. To find the house of your dreams, you will start searching Zillow or Redfin, rather than contacting a real estate agent and asking them to send listings. Will you need the agent to accompany you to the property and “show it” to you? With the open-house option, it became so easy to get inside and just see it in person on your own. So, do you really need somebody who will get 2.5-3% of the purchase price to accompany you on real estate tours?

Buying my own house, I wanted to cut off the unnecessary services and save as much as possible. Realtor commission is typically one of the biggest closing cost expenses. In a traditional home sale, realtor costs can be as much as 5-6% of your home's sale price. That's up to $84,000 on a typical $1,400,000 Bay Area, California home! This is paid by the seller and is typically split evenly across buyer’s and seller’s agent (so over 40k each).

I have heard that the seller pays both real estate agents. So why should I care as a buyer?

Good question! Who do you think pays the seller? It is your money as the buyer which goes to all three parties (the seller and the two real estate agents). You can't control the payment made to the seller’s agent. But you can choose your buyer’s agent wisely to get the maximum cashback.

Can I just go without an agent and get 100% cashback? Why do I need a buyer's agent at all?

If the buyer is without an agent, the seller’s agent gets to keep the whole 5-6% commission. Besides, having an agent is strongly advised. You need to be able to write the offer on the California Association of Realtor (CAR) approved form. You need to read and interpret disclosures. You need to negotiate, take care of inspections, or decide if you want to waive inspections. There is quite a bit of paperwork that goes in purchasing a house as you can imagine.

This would not be ethical even though it’s not illegal. They have already provided you a service and have spent valuable hours driving around with you. They deserve to be compensated. However, if you went to an open house and liked the property, then I will be happy to be your agent and represent you.

Yes, you can switch to another agent at any time unless you have a written contract disallowing this with the previous agent.
